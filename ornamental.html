Ornamental and Aromatic

Snake Plant

This low-maintenance plant is one of the best choices for amateur gardeners or frequent travelers as it can tolerate a wide range of light and water conditions.
Botanical name: Sansevieria trifasciata
Benefits: releases oxygen at night,commonly called the snake plant, because of the shape of its leaves.

Flamingo Flower

With glossy green leaves and rich red blooms, is one of the most stunning plants to grow indoors.
Botanical name: Anthurium
Ornamentation: Its shiny green, heart-shaped, tropical foliage is attractive on its own and adds a blast of greenery to the home

Grape Ivy

Gardenia falls in the favorite list of every houseplant enthusiast for its amazing fragrance and gorgeous white flowers.
Botanical name: Cissus rhombifolia
Ornamentation: This variety has larger leaves than most grape ivy, giving it a bold presence.

Gardenia

Gardenia falls in the favorite list of every houseplant enthusiast for its amazing fragrance and gorgeous white flowers. Gardenias are tropical broadleaf evergreens, usually grown as large indoor potted specimens for the sake of their spectacular scented white flowers.
Botanical name: Crassula ovata
Aromatic: It's natural scents are remarkable, evocative, and memorable.

Gerbera Daisy

The large range of striking flower colors has enabled this flowering pot plant to become a popular house plant choice for a number of years. It blooms repeatedly all-year long.
Botanical name: Gerbera jamesonii
Benefits: releases oxygen at night; filters benzene, formaldehyde and trichloroethylene

Scented Geraniums

They are known for having a delicious, and somewhat overwhelming, aroma. If you brush their leaves or touch them, the plant will quickly release a burst of aroma. When it comes to fragrant indoor plants, this one is easy to grow and comes in many forms. They also offer a variety of health benefits
Botanical name: Pelargonium
Ornamentation: lower stress, reduce inflammation, relieve pain (like migraines)

Lemon Balm

Lemon balm is a perennial herb that grows thick and bushy. If you brush or lightly rub the leaves, you'll find a sweet lemon aroma. You can even pick individual leaves from the plants that you can then use to make teas and dessert. It's important to know that bright light is best for shorter stems and bushy growth. If you use lower light, they'll yield long, lanky stems.
Botanical name: Melissa officinalis
Aromatic: Sweet Lemon aroma, uplifting and relaxing mood

Bunny Ear Cactus

the bunny ears cactus is named after its appearance. It has two pads that are bunny ear shaped. They are covered with glochids or brown prickles and should be handled with care. The bunny ears cactus grows to two or three feet, making it the perfect house plant. It produces white flowers and purple fruits in the summer if exposed to enough light.
Botanical name: Opuntia microdasys
Ornamentation: gardeners dream for their low maintenance and interesting appearance.

Asparagus Fern

The frilly, feathery asparagus fern plant appears soft and fuzzy, but when taking care of asparagus ferns you may be surprised to find they have thorny spurs. Keep the plant well watered in all situations and repot every few years. Care of asparagus ferns indoors involves misting the arching stems to provide humidity to the plants.
Botanical name: Asparagus aethiopicus
Ornamentation: decorating the deck or patio in summer and helping to clean indoor air in winter
