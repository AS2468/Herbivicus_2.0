Roses

A rose is a woody perennial flowering plant of the genus Rosa, in the family Rosaceae, or the flower it bears. There are over three hundred species and thousands of cultivars. They form a group of plants that can be erect shrubs, climbing, or trailing, with stems that are often armed with sharp prickles.
Botanical name: Rosa Indica
Benefits: Boosts focus and clarity

Gotu Kola

Meet the perennial plant that may make you more intelligent. Gotu-Kola is native to Asia and is very popular in Ayurvedic medicine for its benefits on the mind. "At some stage in our lives, we are all worried about memory and cognition.
Botanical name: Centella asiatica
Benefits: Fresh leaves of Gotu-Kola can be eaten in salads and is thought to help sharpen thinking and memory.

Ashwagandha

Used as an adaptogen to help the body be resilient in the face of stress. The root can be used to make a tea, extract or powder and consumed. Ashwagandra grows as a woody evergreen shrub. However, in our gardens we can grow it as an annual.
Botanical name: Withania somnifera
Benefits: helps with anxiety and fatigue.

Kava Plant

Experiencing stress and anxiety? Chill out with the relaxing kava plant. In herbal medicine, it is consumed as a tea or tincture and can be effective in lessening the "edgy" feeling after a long or difficult day.
Botanical name: Piper methysticum
Benefits: used traditionally by people to resolve conflicts and promote relationships in the community.

Shatavari

Shatavari has small pine-needle-like phylloclades (photosynthetic branches) that are uniform and shiny green. In July, it produces minute, white flowers on short, spiky stems. Shatavari is important in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
Botanical name: Asparagus racemosus
Benefits: Brain tonic

Waterhyssop (brahmi)

It is a perennial, creeping herb. The Ayurvedic herb brahmi, also called waterhyssop or bacopa, is believed to sharpen the brain by protecting cells and increasing chemicals associated with learning and memory.
Botanical name: Bacopa Monnieri
Benefits: It's used to treat Alzheimer's disease, anxiety, and ADHD, along with fighting stress and improving memory.

Periwinkle

The periwinkle is a perennial herb with flowers that can bloom throughout the year, depending on the climate. These often are bred for their unique colors, ranging from white to green-yellow and lavender.
Botanical name: Catharanthus roseus
Benefits: increasing blood circulation in the brain, supporting brain metabolism

Kurchi (Kutaj)

A medicinal plant abundantly found in India especially in Himalayan ranges. Its uses are mentioned in the classical Ayurvedic literature and by many folklore claims. References on its use in states of Odisha, Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Andhra Pradesh and Assam are reported and are compiled in this review. The plant is also of extreme economic importance.
Botanical name: Holarrhena antidysenterica
Benefits: Intoxicant, improves memory
