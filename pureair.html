Air Purifier and Humidifier

Peace Lily

It got the recognition it deserves, from the general public after NASA put it on list of “Top Ten Household Air Cleaning Plants.” This tropical shade-loving plant helps cleanse the air we breathe. While we all appreciate cleaner, oxygenated air, it’s also the easy care and resiliency that makes them such popular houseplants.
Botanical name: Spathiphyllum wallisii
Benefits: filters benzene, formaldehyde, trichloroethylene, xylene, toluene and ammonia.

Boston Fern

It is the most popular of all ferns grown indoors and has been found to be one of the easiest to care for and maintain. The Boston displays graceful fronds (leaves) that arch over much, so it's abnormalities have become an advantage. Compared to most other ferns your going to find this plant a lot easier to care for in regards to light, humidity levels and propagating.
Botanical name: Nephrolepis exaltata
Benefits: air humidifier; filters formaldehyde, xylene and toluene

Areca palm

Also known as the butterfly and golden cane palm is the most popular grown indoors.Multiple cane like stems grow from the root system and produce attractive arching fronds, with quite narrow leaflets.As long as your able to provide enough light and warm enough temperatures your palm will thrive wherever you decide to display it.
Botanical name: Dypsis lutescens
Benefits: air humidifier; filters xylene and toluene.

Cornstalk Dracaena

The Warneck Dracaena will combat air pollutants that are released by varnishes and oils.They grow quickly indoors and thrive in any light.Dracaena require less water than most indoor plants. Keep them hydrated by misting the leaves with water and keeping the soil lightly misted (never soggy) as well with good drainage.
Botanical name: Dracaena fragrans
Benefits: filters benzene, formaldehyde and trichloroethylene

Pothos

They do well in bright indirect light as well as low light and can be grown in dry soil or in vases of water. They will thrive in nutrient rich soil, but do almost as well in nutrient poor soil. Pothos plants make a great addition to you bathroom or office because they can tolerate low light.
Botanical name: Epipremnum aureum
Benefits: purify the air of formaldehyde, benzene and carbon monoxide while also helping eliminate odors.

Bamboo Palm

These do best with lots of water and indirect sun or shade, perfect for a room indoors.Potted bamboo palms bring color and warmth to any room in the house. There are many tropical delights to choose from, but most need bright indirect light in order to thrive.
Botanical name: Chamaedorea seifrizii
Benefits: purify the air of dangerous carcinogens such as benzene and trichloroethylene

Rubber Plant

Rubber plants are evergreen trees from India and are easy to grow. If you can pay so much attention to your indoor plants then this one is for you. It easily grows in low light and cooler climates. It’s a very powerful toxin eliminator and air purifier.
Botanical name: Ficus elastica
Benefits: air humidifier, filters formaldehyde

Garden mum

This plant also is from the NASA research, this plant is an air-purifying champion. Popular and very cheap at garden stores, they can be planted outside after they’re finished blooming.
Botanical name: Chrysanthemum morifolium
Flowering season blooms repeatedly all-year long.
Benefits: removes ammonia, benzene, formaldehyde, and xylene from indoor air

Heart-Leaf Philodendron

It is listed by NASA among the best types of indoor plants for purifying air. Philodendrons are considered poisonous, so keep them away from children and pets. This plant prefers moderate water and some sunlight.
Botanical name: Philodendron hederaceum
Benefits: removes formaldehyde, especially higher concentrations

Spider Plant

one of the easiest indoor plants to maintain and a favourite on your journey of discovery with indoor plants. With long slender sword-like leaves that arch from a common central crown, these graceful plants work well in hanging baskets or atop shelves and tables to allow for their trailing habits to be on full display.
Botanical name: Chlorophytum comosum
Benefits: filters formaldehyde, xylene and toluene; produces oxygen in large amount.
